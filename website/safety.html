UPS has a saying: “Safety is our highest priority.” The company invests heavily in training its drivers to operate safely and interact professionally with customers. As a PVD, you are expected to uphold UPS’s safety standards and provide excellent service.

Driving Safety Rules

  • Stay Alert & Avoid Distractions: Keep your eyes moving, constantly scanning for potential hazards. Absolutely no texting or app fiddling while driving – pull over safely if you need to use the app.
  • Obey All Traffic Laws: Observe speed limits and come to complete stops at stop signs. Always wear your seat belt.
  • Safe Following Distance: Maintain a cushion of space between you and the vehicle ahead.
  • Minimize Backing Up: If possible, plan your stops so you can avoid backing your car.
  • Use Your Lights: Since you might deliver into the evening hours, ensure your headlights are on as dusk approaches and use hazard flashers when stopped roadside.
  • Vehicle Condition: Do a quick pre-trip check each day. If something critical fails, stop operations and inform your supervisor.

Package Handling & Personal Safety

Practice proper lifting techniques to protect your back. Be mindful of weather conditions and wear appropriate footwear and gear.

Customer Interaction & Service

  • Professional Appearance & Demeanor: Always wear your UPS uniform or vest during deliveries and keep it visible.
  • Respect Customer Property: Stick to driveways or walkways and close any gates behind you.
  • Handling Signatures and IDs: Follow UPS protocol exactly when a delivery requires a signature or ID check.
  • Difficult Situations: Stay calm if a customer is upset and advise them to contact UPS customer service for further help.
  • Animals and Pets: Treat all unknown dogs as potentially unfriendly and avoid approaching if a dog is aggressive.
  • COVID-19 and Hygiene Protocols: Adhere to any current guidelines regarding masks or contact-free delivery.

Emergencies and Accidents

If you are involved in a vehicle accident or have an injury while on the job, follow UPS emergency protocols and contact your supervisor immediately.

Protecting Packages and Vehicle

While rare, package theft or vehicle break-ins can happen. Keep your vehicle locked whenever possible and park in well-lit areas.

UPS Safety Culture

UPS has a robust safety culture – embrace it. Attend any safety meetings or briefings offered to seasonal drivers.

Summary: Prioritize safety every moment – from how you lift packages to how you drive and how you interact with customers.
